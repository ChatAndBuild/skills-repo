# GLM Output

Support glm output workflows with clear steps and best practices.

## Instruction
- Analyze the GLM summary output, identifying the estimated coefficients (Beta), standard errors, and significance levels (p-values).
- Interpret the effect sizes based on the link function used (e.g., log-odds for Logistic regression or logs for Poisson).
- Assess model fit using diagnostic metrics such as Deviance, AIC (Akaike Information Criterion), and BIC (Bayesian Information Criterion).
- Verify underlying assumptions by checking for overdispersion, residual patterns, and potential multicollinearity (VIF).
- Summarize the hypothesis testing results (e.g., Wald tests or Likelihood Ratio tests) to determine the significance of predictors.
- Formulate a clear research report that translates statistical outputs into actionable biological or business insights.

## When to Use
- When interpreting statistical results from general linear models across various research domains.
- When reporting GLM findings in academic papers or technical documentation with professional rigor.
- When validating the fit and predictive power of a statistical model during experimental analysis.

## Output
- A step-by-step interpretation guide for a specific GLM output table.
- Statistical summary reports containing p-values, odds ratios, and fit indices.
- Recommendations for model refinement or addressing diagnostic issues like poor fit or overdispersion.
