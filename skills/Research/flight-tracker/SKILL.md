# Flight Tracker
## Instruction
- Identify the target flight using callsigns, flight numbers, or aircraft registration details via global flight tracking APIs.
- Monitor real-time ADS-B (Automatic Dependent Surveillance-Broadcast) data to track live altitude, ground speed, and heading.
- Analyze flight paths and trajectories to identify deviations from scheduled routes or potential emergency squawks.
- Correlate flight data with external factors such as weather patterns, air traffic control (ATC) restrictions, and airport congestion.
- Provide step-by-step guidance for tracking international commercial flights through multiple hand-off zones and oceanic corridors.
- Implement best practices for monitoring flight fleets, including managing delay notifications and arrival/departure alerts.

## When to Use
- When needing real-time updates on commercial or private flight status, including estimated time of arrival (ETA) and delays.
- When performing research on historical flight paths or analyzing airport operation efficiency.
- When setting up automated alerts for specific aircraft movements or monitoring air traffic in a particular geographic sector.

## Output
- Real-time flight status summaries containing current position, altitude, speed, and remaining flight time.
- Historical trajectory reports and path visualizations for specific flight missions or research projects.
- Actionable next steps for handling flight delays, including alternative route information and airport facility status.
