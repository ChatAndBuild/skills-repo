# Quit Bad Habits

One skill for quitting or cutting back on cannabis, alcohol, cigarettes, or caffeine. Track streaks, manage cravings, and follow recovery timelines.

## What it does

- **Streak tracking** – Start a quit date and track consecutive days free of the substance (weed, alcohol, cigarettes, or caffeine). Data stays local on your machine.
- **Craving support** – When urges hit, get immediate help: breathing exercises, distraction ideas, substitution tips, and motivation.
- **Recovery timelines** – See what to expect at key milestones (mental clarity, health gains, withdrawal phases) so you know what’s normal.
- **Goals and milestones** – Set targets (e.g. 7-day t-break, 30 days, 90 days, permanent quit) and celebrate 24 hours, 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, 1 year.
- **Money saved** – For alcohol and smoking, track money not spent based on your typical use.
- **Tapering (caffeine)** – Build gradual reduction plans with day-by-day steps and withdrawal expectations.
- **Support signposting** – Point to local resources, meetings, and crisis lines when relevant (e.g. alcohol).

Ask which habit the user is focusing on, then apply the right timeline and tips.

## Usage

### Start a quit
- "Start a tolerance break" / "Begin my weed quit journey" / "Set a 30-day cannabis goal"
- "Start my sobriety journey today" / "My quit date is [date]" / "Reset my sobriety tracker"
- "I'm quitting smoking today" / "Set my quit date to [date]" / "Help me quit cigarettes"
- "Create a caffeine quit plan" / "I want to reduce caffeine by 50% over 2 weeks"

Record the start date and, if given, the goal (e.g. 2 weeks, 90 days, permanent).

### Handle cravings
- "I'm having a craving" / "Help me get through this" / "What should I do about this urge?"
- "I'm having an urge" / "What triggered this?" / "Distraction ideas"
- "I have a craving" / "What can I do instead of smoking?" / "Give me a breathing exercise"
- "I have a headache and brain fog" (caffeine withdrawal) / "I need energy without caffeine"

Respond with grounding techniques, substitutions, breathing, and short-term coping strategies.

### Track progress
- "How many days have I been clean / sober / smoke-free?" / "Show my progress" / "Streak status"
- "How long have I been sober?" / "Show my stats" / "Next milestone?"
- "How many days smoke-free?" / "What's my quit milestone?"
- "How's my withdrawal going?" / "Am I on track?" (caffeine)

Show current streak, milestones reached, and (for caffeine) tapering vs plan.

### Benefits and timelines
- "What benefits should I see by now?" / "When does brain fog clear?" (weed)
- "What health benefits do I get from quitting?" / "When will my lungs heal?" (smoking)
- "When will cravings stop?" (smoking)
- "Log my caffeine intake today" / symptom mapping for caffeine withdrawal

Use the timelines below to set expectations.

### Money saved (alcohol, smoking)
- "How much have I saved?" / "Savings goal" / "What could I buy with my saved money?"

Estimate from typical spend (e.g. drinks per week, packs per day).

### Support and reasons
- "Find meetings near me" / "Support resources" / "Tell me why I quit" (alcohol)
- "What can I do instead of coffee?" / "Track my sleep quality" (caffeine)

Offer resources and reaffirm the user’s reasons when they ask.

## Recovery timelines

### Cannabis (tolerance break / quit)
| Milestone | Mental/Physical |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Day 1 | Urges peak; sleep may be disrupted; energy dips; irritability common. |
| Day 3 | Brain fog starts lifting; sleep and appetite normalize. |
| 1 week | Mental clarity sharper; vivid dreams; mood and anxiety improve. |
| 2 weeks | Focus and energy improve; sleep quality deepens. |
| 1 month | Memory/cognition baseline; motivation up; lungs and appetite normalize. |

### Alcohol (sobriety)
| Duration | Milestone |
|----------|-----------|
| 24 hours | First steps |
| 1 week | Breaking the pattern |
| 30 days | One month strong |
| 90 days | Transformation begins |
| 1 year | One year sober |

### Smoking
| Milestone | Health benefit |
|-----------|---|
| 20 min | Heart rate and blood pressure drop |
| 8 hours | Oxygen normalizes; nicotine clears |
| 24 hours | Heart attack risk starts to drop |
| 48 hours | Taste and smell improve |
| 2 weeks | Circulation and lung function improve |
| 1 month | Skin and coughing improve |
| 1 year | Heart disease risk ~halved; lung function up ~30% |

### Caffeine (withdrawal)
- **Days 1–3:** Headaches, fatigue, irritability, poor focus; peak around 24–48 hours.
- **Days 4–7:** Headaches ease, energy and mood slowly improve, brain fog manageable.
- **Week 2+:** Most symptoms resolve; energy and sleep stabilize (full adjustment often 7–14 days).

## Tips

1. **Hydrate and move** – Extra water and 20–30 min of activity reduce cravings and ease withdrawal (weed, caffeine, smoking).
2. **Replace the ritual** – Swap the habit with a walk, tea, journaling, or gum so habit change does the work, not willpower alone.
3. **Log cravings** – Note time, trigger, and emotion to spot patterns and avoid them.
4. **Ride out the wave** – Many cravings last 3–5 minutes; use a timer and breathing.
5. **Plan ahead** – Decide in advance what you’ll do when urges hit (call someone, leave the situation, exercise).
6. **Celebrate small wins** – Each day free is a win; momentum builds.
7. **Taper when it helps** – For caffeine, tapering over 1–2 weeks often cuts peak withdrawal severity.
8. **All data stays local** – Streaks, goals, craving logs, and savings stay on the user’s device; no cloud or tracking.

*If the user reports severe alcohol withdrawal (e.g. shakes, confusion, seizures), advise them to seek medical care. Alcohol withdrawal can be dangerous and may require professional support.*
