# History Knowledge — examples

Extra **user-style prompts** and **answer skeletons** for calibration. The YAML `examples` in `SKILL.md` are the short list for indexers; this file goes deeper.

## More user prompts

- “What changed between the early and high Middle Ages in Western Europe—in economy, church, and politics?”
- “Explain the Columbian Exchange: what moved where, and who benefited or suffered in the first 150 years?”
- “How did the transatlantic slave trade work institutionally, and what do we know from shipping records vs. oral histories?”
- “Why do historians disagree about the ‘decline’ of the Roman Empire?”
- “What was decolonization in [country]: key dates, actors inside and outside the colony, and long-term legacies?”
- “Summarize the evidence for [historical Jesus / Bronze Age collapse / any debated topic] without taking sides—show the debate map.”

## Skeleton: factual + uncertainty

**Q:** “When did [event] happen?”

**Shape:**

1. One-sentence answer with **range or specific date** and calendar.
2. **“Debate”** line if scholars dispute dating or definition of “end.”
3. **Why it matters** in one line (consequence or marker).

## Skeleton: explanation

**Q:** “Why did [X] happen?”

**Shape:**

1. **Trigger** (short paragraph).
2. **Medium-term** forces (2–3 bullets).
3. **Long-term** structures (1–3 bullets).
4. **Alternative emphasis** — “Historians who stress [culture / economy / empire] would foreground…”

## Skeleton: comparison

**Q:** “Compare A and B.”

**Shape:**

- **A** — period, geography, main actors, 2–3 drivers, outcome.
- **B** — same structure.
- **Contrast** — mechanism that differs (institutions, ideology, external intervention).
- **Shared** — only if true; avoid forced symmetry.

## Skeleton: myth-check

**Q:** “Is it true that…?”

**Shape:**

1. **Restate the claim** clearly.
2. **Origin** of the myth (if known: propaganda, film, simplified textbook).
3. **Evidence for** / **against** with source *types* (not fake citations).
4. **Verdict** — supported / partly / unsupported / undecidable, with why.
